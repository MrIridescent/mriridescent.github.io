## 1. Abstract
The **Psychiatric AI Companion** is a production-grade 2026 framework for **Personality-Aware Systems** and **Explainable Clinical Interventions**. It represents a disruptive niche at the intersection of psychiatric therapy models and neuro-symbolic AI. This suite implements bidirectional synchronization of mental states between a user and their **Cognitive Twin**, utilizing real-time biometric and behavioral data to provide personalized, logically consistent, and clinically valid interventions.

## 2. Research & Operational Foundation
### 2.1 The Personality-Aware Frontier
Traditional psychiatric tools are static. The 2026 **Psychiatric AI Companion** is dynamic, adapting its interventions based on the user's **Big Five** personality vector (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism). Research indicates that personality-aware systems can improve interaction success rates by **24.7%** in therapeutic contexts.

### 2.2 Neuro-Symbolic Explainable Reasoning
A primary bottleneck in medical AI is the "black box" nature of neural networks. This project utilizes a **Neuro-Symbolic architecture** (System 1/2) to provide explainable reasoning for every clinical intervention.
- **System 1 (Neural):** Detects patterns in high-frequency biometrics (HRV, Sleep, Social Interaction).
- **System 2 (Symbolic):** Verifies the neural proposal against a rule-based clinical knowledge base, ensuring every action is logically sound and compliant with medical standards.

### 2.3 The Cognitive Twin Synergy
The companion is designed to work in tandem with the **Cognitive Twin Sync** engine. It utilizes the twin's personality data and memory pruning (Active Forgetting) to maintain a highly accurate, real-time model of the user's mental states.

## 3. Use Cases
### 3.1 Real-World: Personalized CBT Support (2026)
**Scenario:** A user with high openness to experience and an established anxiety disorder is entering a high-stress scenario.
- **Action:** The companion detects a drop in Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and social interaction. It identifies an `ANXIOUS` state and selects a more complex, novel CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) intervention (e.g., a mindfulness guide with a new stimulus reduction protocol) based on the user's high openness.

### 3.2 Fictional: Clinical Intervention for Manic-Depressive Cycle
**Scenario:** A user's sleep metrics drop below 4 hours while their social interactions spike, indicating a potential manic episode.
- **Action:** The system triggers a `MANIC` state alert. It autonomously initiates sleep-hygiene locks on devices and sets financial guardrails (e.g., limits on high-value transactions) to protect the user's long-term well-being, while providing an explainable justification for clinical audit.
