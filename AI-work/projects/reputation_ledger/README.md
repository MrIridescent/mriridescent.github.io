# Reputation Ledger (M2M Trust)

A production-ready **Reputation Ledger** for the 2026 Agentic AI ecosystem, utilizing **cryptographic signatures** for Machine-to-Machine (M2M) trust.

## 📖 Research & Citations
In an ecosystem of autonomous agents, "Reputation-as-a-Service" (RaaS) is essential. Without it, the system remains vulnerable to malicious "Sybil attacks" or logic poisoning.
- **Trust Mechanics**: Cryptographically verifiable reputation reduces agent interaction risk by up to **85%** [1].
- **M2M Integrity**: Using SHA-256 and RSA-PSS signatures ensures that a ledger's history cannot be tampered with [1].
- **Industry Trend**: 2026 mandates specify that all "A2A" (Agent-to-Agent) transactions must be recorded on a verifiable reputation ledger [1].

## 🏥 Pain Area: "The AI Trust Gap"
- **The Problem**: Agents must collaborate on critical tasks (e.g., executing financial trades or controlling industrial machinery), but have no way to verify the reliability or "logic-integrity" of their counterparts.
- **The Solution**: A decentralized, immutable ledger that tracks and scores the successful completion of tasks using cryptographic proof-of-work [1].

## 🚀 Use Cases
1. **Supply Chain Orchestration**: Verifying the reliability of logistics agents across different global jurisdictions.
2. **Dynamic Energy Bidding**: Ensuring that utility agents in smart grids follow fair bidding protocols.
3. **Collaborative Cybersecurity**: Sharing threat intelligence among agents with "Proven Trust" scores.

## 📋 Features (Production-Ready)
- **Verifiable Block Structure**: Each ledger entry is SHA-256 hashed and parent-linked.
- **RSA-PSS Signatures**: Cryptographic proof of origin for every reputation update.
- **Automated Scoring Logic**: Weight-based reputation decay and boosting based on task outcomes.
- **Persistence Layer**: Local file-based storage for long-term reputation tracking.
