## **1. Abstract / Research Summary**

The **M2M Social Contract Negotiator** is an autonomous agentic framework designed to manage Machine-to-Machine (M2M) trust, negotiation, and social contracts. In the 2026 AI landscape, as agents begin to interact, negotiate, and transact autonomously via **Agentic Commerce Protocols (ACP)**, "trust" has become the primary bottleneck for structural integration.

This project implements a **Machine-to-Machine (M2M) Social Contract** system that uses verifiable performance metrics and machine-signed mandates to determine which agents are allowed to "hire" other sub-agents in a **Dynamic Mixture of Experts (DMoE)** environment. Research indicates that cryptographically verifiable reputation systems can reduce agent interaction risk by up to 85%, moving the industry from human-led audits to automated, machine-signed reputation ledgers.

## **2. Operational Documents & Technical Specifications**

### **System Objectives**
- **Autonomous Negotiation:** Enable agents to negotiate contract terms (SLA, Budget, Task) with zero human intervention.
- **Machine-Signed Mandates:** Use digital signatures to verify the origin and integrity of every M2M contract.
- **Verifiable Performance Metrics:** Implement a scoring system that decay or boost an agent's reputation based on contract fulfillment.

### **Architecture Overview**
The platform utilizes a **Goal-Oriented Reasoning loop** where an agent interprets high-level goals and determines the necessary sub-tasks and counterpart agents required for fulfillment. Negotiations are conducted via **JSON-RPC 2.0** bidirectional handshakes, and all finalized contracts are recorded on a verifiable reputation ledger (e.g., using **SHA-256** and **RSA-PSS** signatures).
