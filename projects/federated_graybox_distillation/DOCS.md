## **1. Abstract / Research Summary**

The **Federated Gray-Box Distillation** project is a collaborative training protocol designed for the open-source community to share the benefits of frontier-grade distillation without sacrificing proprietary data sovereignty. In the 2026 "Year of Truth," the AI industry has recognized that the cost of training massive teacher models is unsustainable for individual organizations.

This project implements a **Gray-Box** approach, where organizations collaborate on a global student model by sharing obfuscated gradients and reasoning pathways rather than raw data. Research shows that this collaborative training method allows smaller student models (e.g., 7B parameter models) to replicate 95% of the performance of frontier-grade teachers (e.g., GPT-5 class) while keeping sensitive training data local and secure.

## **2. Operational Documents & Technical Specifications**

### **System Objectives**
- **Collaborative Training:** Allow multiple organizations to co-train a global student model.
- **Data Sovereignty:** Ensure raw proprietary data never leaves the local organization's server.
- **Reasoning Distillation:** Replicate the teacher's logical inference pathways in the student model.

### **Architecture Overview**
The platform utilizes a **Teacher-Student architecture** within a federated framework. The teacher model (e.g., a massive frontier model) provides the reasoning logic for a given data sample. The local node then generates an obfuscated gradient update that encapsulates this reasoning without revealing the underlying data. These updates are synchronized across the community of collaborators via a secure, encrypted handshake.
