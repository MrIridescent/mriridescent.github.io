## **1. Abstract / Research Summary**

The **Neuromorphic Edge Core** is a sub-microsecond inference architecture designed to mimic the human brain's energy efficiency and asynchronous processing. In the 2026 "Year of Truth," the demand for real-time autonomy in robotics and high-frequency trading has pushed traditional Von Neumann architectures to their limits.

This project implements a **Spiking Neural Network (SNN)** simulation that processes information only when "spikes" (significant events) occur in the data stream. Research indicates that neuromorphic computing can achieve a **90% reduction in data center energy use** and provide sub-microsecond latency (approx. 500ns) for critical decision-making in edge environments.

## **2. Operational Documents & Technical Specifications**

### **System Objectives**
- **Sub-microsecond Latency:** Achieve inference speeds below 1 microsecond (1000ns).
- **Extreme Energy Efficiency:** Reduce energy consumption to the picojoule (pJ) level per synaptic event.
- **Asynchronous Processing:** Eliminate the need for a global clock, allowing for event-driven intelligence.

### **Architecture Overview**
The core utilizes an asynchronous spiking logic where neurons only consume energy when their activation threshold is met. This is particularly effective for processing high-frequency sensory data (Vision, LiDAR, Audio) in autonomous vehicles and humanoid robotics, where constant cloud callbacks are impossible due to latency constraints.
