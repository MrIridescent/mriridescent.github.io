## **Core Operational Logic**
1.  **Liquid Time-Constants (Tau)**: Each cell dynamically adjusts its "processing speed" based on the input signal. If the input is stable, the time-constant slows down to conserve energy; if a sudden anomaly occurs, the cell accelerates to perform high-fidelity reasoning.
2.  **Euler Discretization**: The continuous-time differential equations are solved using optimized Euler discretization in the forward pass, ensuring C-level performance on fixed-point hardware.
3.  **On-Device Personalization**: LFMs are designed to fine-tune their internal time-constants locally, allowing the model to adapt to a specific user's biometrics or a factory's vibration patterns without data ever leaving the device.
